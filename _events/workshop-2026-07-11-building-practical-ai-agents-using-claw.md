## Overview

Join NVIDIA, the University of Sheffield Centre for Machine Intelligence (CMI) and Research Software Engineering (RSE),
and the N8 consortium for the "Building Practical AI Agents Using Claw: Hands-On Workshop". This one-day workshop
introduces participants to AI agents using Claw through a practical mix of short talks, guided setup, hands-on
exploration, and open workshop time. Participants will learn how AI agents can use tools, files, APIs, models, and
workflows, then use NVIDIA Brev credits to get started quickly.

The day is designed to be practical and collaborative. Participants will work in teams on use cases for research,
education, productivity, operations, accessibility, or other real-world workflows, then share their progress in a short
show-and-tell session.

## What Participants Will Learn

- What AI agents are and why tool-using workflows matter.
- How OpenClaw supports practical agent workflows.
- Where NVIDIA NemoClaw and OpenShell fit into safer agent development.
- How to use NVIDIA Brev to access a development environment quickly.
- How to move from an idea to a simple prototype or workflow.

## Participant Notes

- Bring: laptop, charger, GitHub account, and an idea for a useful agent workflow.
- Lunch and refreshments will be provided.
- Credits: NVIDIA Brev credits will be provided to participating individuals at the end so they can continue exploring
  and developing their workflows after the event.
- Data guidance: avoid using sensitive, confidential, personal, or unpublished data during the workshop unless
  explicitly approved.
